# Renewable_Energy
Load profile analysis of DK2 region of Denmark

# ABSTRACT
Global energy demand surged in 2024, surpassing historical growth trends and driving increased
consumption across all major energy sources. Achieving net-zero emissions by 2050 requires rapid
expansion of renewable energy. However, the inherent intermittency of sources such as wind and
solar poses significant challenges for maintaining a stable and continuous power supply. As
demand rises, conventional systems struggle to balance generation and consumption in real time
due to temporal mismatches. Energy Storage Systems (ESS) play a pivotal role in mitigating
imbalance by storing surplus energy during peak production and delivering it during lowgeneration
periods, thereby enhancing grid stability and flexibility. As variety Energy Storage
Technologies (EST) exist, choosing an appropriate technology is a key challenge. While EST
selection is inherently multi-parameter and should be based on actual need, this study proposes
optimal storage technology selection based on the variability in RE production and load patterns.
Analysis is focused on the DK1 pricing area in Denmark and incorporates production forecasts of
upcoming renewable projects planned by Herning kommune. Net load analysis is performed on
both current and forecasted load profiles, including planned renewable generation data. Seasonal
trends derived from these profiles are used to assess variability and identify optimal storage
technologies. This work demonstrates how preliminary analysis can guide storage technology
selection to meet the specific requirements of renewable energy producers and grid operators
thereby promote balance between supply and demand.
